Jace Sikora - Fettucine Alfredo

Ingredients

  • 1.5 pounds dry fettuccine pasta
  • 1 cup butter
  • ¾ pint heavy cream
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 dash garlic salt
  • ¾ cup grated Romano cheese
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese

History

Fettuccine alfredo originates in Rome where it was first invented in the early 20th century by Alfredo Di Lelio. Fettuccine Alfredo is a pasta dish made with fettuccine, butter, and parmesan cheese. The dish is now popular in other countries like the U.S. where cream is used to thicken the sauce. Additionally other ingredients like chicken, shrimp, or broccoli are commonly added to the dish. In Italy these practices are not common.
This recipe makes a fettuccine alfredo dish in a non-traditional style because it uses heavy cream.

Directions

  1. Gather Ingredients
  2. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil.
  3. Add fettuccine and cook for 8 to 10 minutes or until al dente; drain.
  4. Melt butter into cream in a large saucepan over low heat.
  5. Add salt, pepper, and garlic salt.
  6. Increase the heat to medium.
  7. Stir in grated Romano and Parmesan cheese until melted and sauce has thickened.
  8. Add cooked pasta to sauce and toss until thoroughly coated; serve immediately.
